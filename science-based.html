    The final report of the study on projections of lower extremity amputations among people with diabetes was received in October 2004. Using national survey data and Medicare data on amputations, this study used various statistical models to develop projections of lowerextremity amputation (LEA). These analyses suggested that the number of persons with diabetes experiencing amputations is likely to grow rapidly between 2002 and 2050 among those less than 75 years of age. However, these projections of lower extremity amputations varied widely by the assumptions used to model the projections.
