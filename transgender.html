    HIV prevention is cost-saving to society. The estimated lifetime costs of treating a single person with HIV infection is $402,000. We now know more than ever about how to prevent HIV among high-risk populations and preserve the health of those infected. CDC has also employed high-impact strategies for its program for young men of color who have sex with men and young transgendered persons, requiring all funded organizations to meet minimum testing targets, HIV-positivity rates, and linkage to care rates for HIV-positive individuals.
